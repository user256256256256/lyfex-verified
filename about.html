Our Founder's Journey:

Meet Nanteza Nadia Malker, the driving force behind LYFEX AFRICA. Nadia Malker embarked on this journey with a deep understanding of the root causes of malnutrition, a knowledge that was cultivated through a unique blend of experiences.

As former business development personnel in a financial institution, Nadia Malker witnessed firsthand the profound impact of poverty on families. It became evident that economic challenges were often intertwined with nutritional struggles. Families facing financial constraints were more susceptible to inadequate nutrition, creating a cycle that affected generations.

This awareness only deepened during Nadia Malker's tenure as a nutritionist in various hospitals. From high-income earners to those with moderate and low incomes, Nadia Malker encountered a diverse range of patients. What became glaringly apparent was that, regardless of financial background, ignorance played a significant role in perpetuating malnutrition.

In the corridors of hospitals and financial institutions alike, Nadia Malker recognized a shared challenge: the urgent need to bridge the gap between nutrition knowledge and practical solutions, especially for families facing economic hardships.

The Birth of LYFEX AFRICA:

Fueled by the desire to create meaningful change, Nadia Malker founded LYFEX AFRICA with a mission deeply rooted in integrity, education, health, empowerment, and family—the core values that form the heartbeat of our services.

Today, LYFEX AFRICA stands as a beacon of hope and support for families navigating the complexities of nutrition, particularly those impacted by poverty and limited access to knowledge. We've developed personalized nutrition therapy services, integrating the concept of 'NutriFintrancy'—nutrition financial literacy—to empower families to make informed and affordable choices especially for their mothers and infants.

Through our journey, we've learned that it's not just about providing nutritional guidance; it's about empowering families to break free from the cycle of malnutrition, fostering health, and building a foundation for a brighter future.
